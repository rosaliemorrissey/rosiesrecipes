Nana's Cookies

Mary Morrissey proudly worked as a nurse while raising her five kids in Dedham, MA. Mary excelled in the kitchen and was always the one to have family recipes avaliable. She even went so far as to record other family recipes from family members who only measured by eye.

Always looking for new recipes to try, Mary often referred to the Boston Globe's "Confidential Chat" a column that had a majority female readership. The column covered a variety of topics including stain removal, child rearing, and recipes! While no one is 100% certain were the recipe came from, it is a favorite in the family, and while we all try no one can make them quite like Nana could.

I was very lucky to spend time with my Nana during my childhood. A day at Nana's always contained two things, a craft and a snack. I hope you enjoy these cookies that mean so much to my family and always bring us joy!

Ingredients

1 cup butter or vegetable oil

2 eggs

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

1 cup light brown sugar

1 cup sugar

1 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon baking soda

2 cups flour

2 cups quick oats

2 cups rice krispys

1 cup shredded coconut

Directions

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F with a rack in the center position.

In large bowl, stir together flour, quick oats, rice krispys, and coconut in a large bowl.

In the large bowl, mix in butter, eggs, and vanilla extract.

After mixing, stir in brown and white sugars, salt, and baking soda.

Bake until cookies are light brown, approximately 8-14 minutes depending on your oven.

Happy Baking!
