Banana Bread

Ingredients

Batter

2 cups mashed Banana (about 5 medium bananas)

0.5 vegetable oil

1 cup light brown sugar, packed

2 large eggs

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

2 cup all-purpose flour

1 teaspoon baking soda

0.5 teaspoon baking powder

0.75 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon cinnamon

0.5 cup chopped walnutes*

*Leave the nuts out, if desiredd; or substitute up to 1 cup chopped dried fruit, chocolate or butterscotch chips, sunfower or pumpkin seeds, or other mix-ins of your choice.

Topping

1 tablespoon granuated sugar

0.5 teaspoon cinnamon

Directions

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F with a rack in the center position. Lightly grease a 9"x5" loaf pan; if your pan is glass or stoneware, reduce the oven temperature to 325 degree F.

In large bowl, stir together the mashed banana, oil, sugar, eggs, and vanilla.

Weigh your flours; or measure them by gently spooning into a cup, then sweeping off any excess. Mix the flours, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and chopped walnuts into the banana mixture. Scrape the bottom and sides of the bowl and mix again to thoroughly combine the ingredients.

Scoop the batter into the prepared pan. Mix together the sugar and cinnamon, and sprinke over the batter.

Bake the bread for about 60-75 minutes, until the bread feels set on the top, and a paring knife inserted in the center comes out clean, or with just a few moist crumbs (but no wet batter). If you have a digital thermometer, the bread's temperature at the center should register about 205 degrees F. If the bread appears to be browning too quickly, tent it with aluminum foil for the final 15 to 20 minutes of baking.

Remove the bread from the oven. Cool it in the pan for 15 minutes, then loosen the edges, and turn it out of the pan onto a rack to cool completely.

Storage information: Store leftover bread, tighty wrapped, at room temperature for several days. Freeze for longer storage.

Happy Baking!
